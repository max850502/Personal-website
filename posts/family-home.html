What Does “Home” Mean?

Chapter 1 · Family

Home used to be a place that felt stable and familiar — a physical location tied to daily routines and memories.

But as life gradually stretched across Taiwan and the Bay Area, that definition began to shift. I started to notice that the feeling of “home” was no longer anchored to a single location.

Instead, it followed the people I cared about, the conversations that felt effortless, and the moments that brought a sense of quiet belonging.

Home became less about where I lived, and more about where I could return to — emotionally, not geographically.
