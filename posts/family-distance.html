The Distance Between Two Lives

Chapter 1 · Family

Living between two places creates a subtle but constant tension.

Part of life unfolds in Taiwan, while another part continues in the Bay Area. Each place carries its own rhythm, expectations, and relationships.

At times, it feels like being split between two versions of life — never fully present in either, yet deeply connected to both.

Over time, I began to understand that distance is not only about separation. It also creates perspective. It forces clarity about what matters, and what can be let go.
